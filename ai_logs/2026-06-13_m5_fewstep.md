# 2026-06-13 - M5 few-step DMD2 student (beat M3 on off-support)

## Goal
M4 (one-step DMD2) landed approximately equal to M3: distribution matching had
already solved the toy on ED/MMD (~1e-3, at the noise floor), and the GAN never
really contributed. The one axis with real, explainable headroom was the
**off-support fraction** (edge-smoothing of the hard checkerboard boundaries):
M3 one-step ~0.158, M4 one-step ~0.137, multi-step teacher ~0.124.

Hypothesis: a **few-step (4-step) student** has strictly more capacity than a
one-step map from a Gaussian, so it should carve sharper boundaries and clearly
beat the one-step students on off-support while keeping all 8 modes. This
implements the two DMD2 contributions that were still missing: the multi-step
generator (sec 4.4) and backward simulation (sec 4.5).

- Cursor (Claude) agent: plan-mode review of the repo + papers, then implementation.

## Method (DMD2 sec 4.4 + 4.5, adapted to 2D)
- **Schedule:** 4 steps at timesteps `[199, 149, 99, 49]` for T=200 (mirrors the
  paper's 999,749,499,249 for T=1000).
- **Inference (sec 4.4):** from `z ~ N(0,I)`, alternate one denoise
  (`x0 = predict_x0(x_t, t, student(x_t, t))`) and one renoise
  (`q_sample(x0, t_next)`); return the final `x0`. A single-entry schedule
  `[T-1]` reduces exactly to the one-step generator, so the same code drives the
  1-step ablation arm.
- **Backward simulation (sec 4.5):** during training, pick a scheduled step
  uniformly, run the inference loop up to that step under `no_grad` to build the
  student-generated input `x_{t_i}`, then take one **differentiable** denoise
  `G(x_{t_i}, t_i)` and apply the existing DMD surrogate + GAN loss. This removes
  the train/inference input mismatch (the generator is trained on its own
  intermediate samples, not on noised real data).
- **Reuse:** `dmd.dmd_generator_loss`, `dmd2.disc_loss`/`gen_gan_loss`/
  `disc_diagnostics`, and the TTUR `mu_fake` update are reused verbatim. No
  architecture change (the student already conditions on `t`).
- **Init choice (deviation from the plan, documented):** student and `mu_fake`
  init from the **EMA teacher** (DMD2 inits the generator from the pretrained
  diffusion model), not from the one-step M3/M4 student. A multi-step student must
  denoise competently at every scheduled timestep, not just `t=T-1`; the one-step
  students were only ever trained at `t=199`, so teacher-EMA init is the safer,
  more faithful starting point. Configurable via `init_student_checkpoint`.

## Result (vs true pi_1, fixed eval noise)
| model | steps | modes | off-support | ED | MMD |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| M3 best student | 1 | 8 | 0.158 | 0.0059 | 0.0022 |
| M4 DMD2 student | 1 | 8 | 0.137 | 0.0088 | 0.0029 |
| M1 multi-step teacher | 200 | 8 | 0.124 | 0.0053 | 0.0014 |
| **M5 DMD2 student** | **4** | **8** | **0.064** | 0.0060 | 0.0027 |

**Clear win.** M5 cuts off-support ~2.5x vs M3 (0.158 -> 0.064) and drops it
**below the multi-step teacher**, with all 8 modes (entropy 0.996) and ED/MMD a
tie within noise. Robust across evals: off-support 0.058-0.066 at n=2000 (2 seeds)
and n=5000.

## Ablation (off-support, all arms matched: teacher-EMA init, 3000 steps)
| variant | steps | GAN | TTUR | off-support | ED |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 4-step, +GAN (fu=3) [M5] | 4 | yes | fu=3 | 0.064 | 0.0060 |
| 4-step, no GAN (fu=3) | 4 | no | fu=3 | 0.071 | 0.0171 |
| 4-step, no GAN, fu=1 (-TTUR) | 4 | no | fu=1 | 0.080 | 0.0143 |
| 1-step, no GAN (fu=3) | 1 | no | fu=3 | 0.144 | 0.0044 |
| 1-step, +GAN (fu=3) | 1 | yes | fu=3 | 0.148 | 0.0058 |

Takeaways: off-support is governed mostly by **step count** (1-step ~0.145 ->
4-step ~0.064-0.080, a ~2x cut). TTUR helps the 4-step student (fu=3 < fu=1), and
**the GAN finally contributes** once there is headroom: at 4 steps it both lowers
off-support (0.071 -> 0.064) and clearly cuts ED (0.0171 -> 0.0060) - the opposite
of the inert GAN we saw in M4. The 1-step rows keep the lowest ED but cannot
sharpen boundaries; few-step trades a tiny ED cost for a large off-support gain.
