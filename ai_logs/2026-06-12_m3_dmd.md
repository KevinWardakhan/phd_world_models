## Design choices / DMD adaptation
- Generator `G_theta(z) = predict_x0(z, t_gen, student(z, t_gen))` at `t_gen = T-1`.
- KL gradient `grad_x = a_t (s_fake - s_real)` implemented as a detached
  dot-product surrogate `(x * grad_x.detach()).sum()/n`, so autograd wrt theta
  equals the DMD generator gradient. Scores from eps via `s = -eps/sigma_t`.
- Real score = frozen EMA teacher; fake score = `mu_fake` trained online on
  detached student samples with the standard DDPM eps-loss. Both detached during
  the generator step so neither the teacher nor `mu_fake` is updated by it.
- **Kept the teacher architecture and time embedding in the student** (deviation
  from DMD's time-free one-step generator) so the student/`mu_fake` are
  initialized from the EMA teacher and a few-step path stays open. The student is
  always called at the fixed `t_gen`, so it acts as a one-step generator.
- **LPIPS -> MSE** for the regression loss (LPIPS is meaningless in 2D);
  SmoothL1/L1 are config options. Regression targets `y_ref` are produced by a
  **deterministic DDIM eta=0** sampler and the `(z_ref, y_ref)` pairs are cached
  at `outputs/m3/reg_pairs.pt`.
- Ablation: `dmd_reg` (MSE, 1 fake update), `dmd_no_reg` (none, 1), and
  `dmd_no_reg_ttur` (none, 5 fake updates). Hypothesis tested: does TTUR recover
  the stability/quality of regression-based DMD when regression is removed?
- Reuses the shared fixed `x_T` (`outputs/eval_noise.pt`) and the M1/M2 metrics
  and plotting; M1/M2 outputs untouched; M3 writes only under `outputs/m3/`.

## What was checked
- `./run.sh m3` runs clean from the existing checkout (CPU) and writes per-variant
  artifacts plus `outputs/m3/summary_metrics.{json,md}`.
- Metrics vs true `pi_1` (n=2000, `T=200`, one-step student):
  - M2 one-step teacher: 7 modes, entropy 0.558, off-support 0.443, ED 0.311, MMD 0.244.
  - `dmd_reg`: 8 modes, entropy 0.999, off-support 0.147, ED 0.0070, MMD 0.0022.
  - `dmd_no_reg` (best): 8 modes, entropy 0.999, off-support 0.180, ED 0.0055, MMD 0.0024.
  - `dmd_no_reg_ttur`: 8 modes, entropy 0.998, off-support 0.164, ED 0.0080, MMD 0.0022.
- Best variant by energy distance: `dmd_no_reg`; beats M2 one-step: True.

## Honest takeaway
All DMD variants recover 8/8 modes and roughly match the multi-step teacher's
distances while sampling in one step, a ~40-55x ED/MMD improvement over the M2
one-step baseline. On this simple 2D toy, regression was not necessary (pure
distribution matching was stable and best), and TTUR neither helped nor hurt
much; the DMD2 instability that motivates them does not appear here. Those tools
are expected to matter on harder targets (M4).
