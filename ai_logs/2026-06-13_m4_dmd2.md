# 2026-06-13 - M4 DMD2 Stage B

## Goal
Implement DMD2 (Yin et al., 2024) on the 2D toy: continue training the best M3
student (no regression, TTUR `fake_updates_per_gen=3`) with a GAN loss added on
noised samples, regression fully dropped. Produce one main DMD2 model and compare

## DMD2 choices
- **Regression dropped** entirely (no `(z_ref, y_ref)`, no MSE/L1/smooth_l1).
- **TTUR kept** at `fake_updates_per_gen=3` (the M3.1-selected best).
- **DMD surrogate reused** unchanged from `src/dmd.py` (`grad_x = a_t (s_fake - s_real)`, detached).
- **GAN on noised samples**: `F(x,t) = q_sample(x,t,eps)` over the full schedule
  `t ~ U[0,T)`. Real `x_real ~ pi_1`, fake `x_fake = G(z)` both diffused before `D`.
- **Discriminator = separate small MLP** `D(x_t,t) -> logit` with the denoiser's
  sinusoidal time embedding. The paper attaches a head to `mu_fake`'s bottleneck;
  with a tiny 2D MLP that sharing buys nothing, so a separate critic is the toy
  simplification. Raw logits + softplus losses (no sigmoid).
- Losses: `L_D = softplus(-D(F(real,t))) + softplus(D(F(fake.detach(),t)))`;
  `L_GAN_G = softplus(-D(F(fake,t)))`; `L_G = L_DMD + 0.1 * L_GAN_G`.
- Init: student + `mu_fake` from `outputs/m3/dmd_no_reg_ttur/{student,mu_fake}.pt`
  (mu_fake -> EMA teacher if missing); teacher frozen EMA; discriminator random.
- Guards: teacher frozen; `mu_fake`/`D` excluded from the generator optimizer;
  score diff detached; fake detached for `mu_fake`/`D` updates, not for the
  generator step.

## What was checked
- `./run.sh m4` runs clean on CPU (~2.5 min, 3000 steps) and writes all expected
  artifacts under `outputs/m4/`.
- Metrics vs true `pi_1` (n=2000, one-step student):
  - M3 best (no-reg TTUR): 8 modes, entropy 0.998, off-support 0.158, ED 0.0060, MMD 0.0027.
  - **M4 DMD2**: 8 modes, entropy 0.997, off-support 0.145, ED 0.0073, MMD 0.0031.
- `m4_improves_over_m3` (energy distance): False; MMD: False; off-support: improved.
- Training curves: `L_D ~ 1.386 (2 ln2)`, `L_GAN_G ~ 0.693 (ln2)` flat for the
  whole run -> discriminator at its trivial equilibrium (logits ~ 0).

## Honest takeaway
DMD2 **preserves** M3's strong result: all 8 modes, balanced entropy, no
destabilization, and a small off-support improvement (0.158 -> 0.145). But ED/MMD
do **not** improve (slightly worse), because the GAN is nearly inactive here: once
the M3 student already matches `pi_1` and samples are noised over the full
schedule, the discriminator cannot separate real from fake and sits at its
trivial fixed point, so the adversarial gradient is weak. This is a faithful
negative-ish result on an already-solved toy; a visible GAN effect would need a
larger `lambda_gan`, higher disc lr, or low-noise-only GAN timesteps. Per the
spec (one main model, no sweep) we report it honestly rather than tuning to force
a win.
